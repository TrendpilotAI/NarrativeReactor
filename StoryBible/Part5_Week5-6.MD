## CONCEPT

## SCENE 1: THE INSTINCT | 0:00-0:20

Jamie's desk. Three monitors. They're moving fast, typing a query into Signal Studio with confidence.

**JAMIE:** (quieter) "Oh."

# ★ EPISODE 6.1: "THE NEW STANDARD" ★

## FINALE PIECE

**Format:** 120-second cinematic finale  
**Platforms:** ALL PLATFORMS | Paid promotion  
**Posting:** Monday, 8:00 AM EST

---

## CONCEPT

Maya delivers a keynote speech that synthesizes everything we've learned. The audience includes all our characters—Marcus, Elena, Jamie, Helen—each transformed in their own way. This is the culmination: from one advisor's midnight desperation to an industry's new direction.

---

## SCENE 1: THE GATHERING | 0:00-0:30

### SETTING
Conference stage. A major industry event. Hundreds of attendees in polished rows. Stage lighting is warm but professional—the lighting of authority, not entertainment.

Maya walks to the podium. She's different now—not the exhausted advisor from Episode 1.1, but someone who has fought for something and won.

### CAMERA
CRANE SHOT descending from above, revealing the scope of the audience. 

### INTERCUT
Quick shots of our characters in the audience:
- **Marcus** — no longer skeptical, nodding with pride, his father's watch visible on his wrist
- **Elena** — taking notes, already thinking about implementation
- **Jamie** — filming on their phone, ready to share
- **Helen Murdoch** — distinguished in the front row, a client who became an advocate

Notably absent: Richard Sterling. His seat empty—his choice, made with dignity.

### CAMERA
SLOW PUSH-IN on Maya at the podium. She touches her jade pendant. Begins.

---

## SCENE 2: THE SPEECH | 0:30-1:30

### MAYA'S ADDRESS

**MAYA:** "Three years ago, I lost a client I should have saved."

The room quiets. This is not the opening they expected.

**MAYA:** "Not to death—to a market event I saw coming. I circled it on a printout at 2 AM, three weeks before it happened. But by the time I could compile the research, cross-reference the data, build a case that would survive a board meeting..."

She pauses. The audience is still.

**MAYA:** "...twenty-three percent of her retirement was gone. Not because I didn't know. Because I couldn't prove what I knew fast enough."

### INTERCUT
Flash of Episode 1.5 footage—Maya's flashback, Eleanor's face falling.

**MAYA:** "That experience broke something in me. It also built something."

### CAMERA
WIDER SHOT as Maya gains momentum.

**MAYA:** "Today, our industry stands at a crossroads. We can continue drowning in data while our clients wait for decisions. We can keep working eighty-hour weeks to provide service that should take forty. We can watch our best people burn out doing work that machines should handle while the work only humans can do—the connection, the wisdom, the trust—goes undone."

Beat.

**MAYA:** "Or we can embrace a new standard."

### INTERCUT
Montage of campaign moments:
- The 11-second answer appearing on screen
- Marcus's face as he witnesses it
- Elena approving the audit trail
- Helen Murdoch's satisfied nod
- The coalition forming around the conference table

**MAYA:** "We call it Decision Velocity. And it's not about replacing human judgment. It's about giving human judgment the speed it deserves."

She leans forward.

**MAYA:** "Eleven seconds from question to answer. Eleven seconds from data to decision. Eleven seconds from insight to action."

### CAMERA
CLOSE on Maya. Full conviction.

**MAYA:** "Some people will tell you that's impossible. That you can't be fast and thorough. That you can't be innovative and compliant. That you can't use AI and still be human."

She smiles.

**MAYA:** "They're wrong. And they're about to be obsolete."

---

## SCENE 3: THE MONTAGE | 1:30-1:50

### VISUAL
As Maya continues speaking, we see a final montage:

- Maya's jade pendant catching stage light
- Marcus checking his phone—message from his daughter: "Can't wait for dinner tonight, Dad"
- Elena shaking hands with a regulator, confident smile
- Jamie and an older colleague solving a problem together
- Helen Murdoch signing a referral for a new client to Thompson Partners
- An empty desk—Richard Sterling's nameplate being removed, but a new photo in its place: him on a sailboat, at peace
- Signal Studio interfaces glowing on screens across the industry

### VOICEOVER (Maya, continuing speech)

*"The future of our industry belongs to those who can act on what they know—instantly, transparently, confidently. That future starts now. That future starts with a question you can ask in natural language and answer in eleven seconds."*

---

## SCENE 4: THE CLOSE | 1:50-2:00

### CAMERA
EXTREME CLOSE-UP on Maya's eye—echoing Episode 1.1, but transformed. The reflection shows not chaos, but clarity. Not cold blue screens, but warm audience light.

### MAYA (final line)

"The gap between knowing and proving used to cost us everything. Now it costs us eleven seconds."

She straightens.

"Signal Studio. This is the new standard."

### CAMERA
PULL BACK to show the entire auditorium rising in applause. Our characters on their feet.
